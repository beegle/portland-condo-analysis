Recovery Signals

What to Watch

Positive Signals

  • ▲ Violent crime down 17% (H1 2025)
  • ▲ Homicides down 51% — steepest drop among 68 major cities
  • ▲ Foot traffic at 86% of pre-pandemic levels
  • ▲ Mortgage rates improved (6.00% vs. 6.63% a year ago)
  • ▲ Condo development pipeline collapsed 90% — no future supply
  • ▲ Only 656 multifamily units in pipeline (lowest since 2011)

Headwinds

  • ▼ 34% CBD office vacancy (among highest nationally)
  • ▼ 59,000 fewer daytime workers vs. 2019
  • ▼ Pearl District PPSF down 13.26% YoY
  • ▼ 972 active listings (up from 831 in 2024)
  • ▼ Avg DOM 130 days (up 132% YoY)
  • ▼ Oregon tax stack $30–40K/yr for high earners

Key Inflection Points

  • ● Office vacancy projected to peak end of 2026
  • ● Mortgage rate forecast: ~5.9% by EOY 2026
  • ● Remote work patterns stabilizing nationally
  • ● FLOCK food hall + ground retail maturation
  • ● Ritz-Carlton hotel occupancy/reputation building
  • ● Supply pipeline empty = potential scarcity by 2028–30

The Bottom Line on Timing

The most likely near-term path for Portland luxury condos is gradual normalization, not a sharp rebound. The structural headwinds — office vacancy, negative perception, tax burden — are real and unlikely to fully resolve in 2–3 years. But the supply pipeline has shut off, crime metrics are improving sharply, and rates have moved in the right direction.

For a buyer who plans to hold 7–10 years and genuinely wants to use the unit: the per-square-foot values available today may not recur. For a buyer seeking near-term appreciation or pure investment returns, the math remains challenging. The optimal window to negotiate is now through mid-2026, while Ready Capital is under maximum pressure to move inventory before office vacancy peaks.
