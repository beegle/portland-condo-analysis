Composite Assessment

Overall Risk: Moderate-High

The weighted composite risk score is 6.8 out of 10. This reflects a genuine opportunity paired with significant structural risks. The highest-weighted factors are HOA/carrying cost risk and liquidity risk — both driven by the building's 91.7% unsold inventory and Portland's broader market challenges.

For a patient buyer with a 7–10 year horizon who can absorb the carrying costs, many of these risks are manageable. For a buyer seeking near-term appreciation or income, the risk profile is unfavorable.

Action Items

Due Diligence Checklist

Before Making an Offer

  • ☐ Request and review HOA financials (budget, actuals, reserves)
  • ☐ Obtain reserve study — verify funding level
  • ☐ Confirm status of $7.8M inclusionary housing fee
  • ☐ Review master insurance policy limits and deductibles
  • ☐ Verify parking — deeded vs. assigned, number of spaces
  • ☐ Tour the unit in person — assess spatial flow and storage

Before Closing

  • ☐ Engage Oregon real estate attorney for HOA review
  • ☐ Obtain HO-6 insurance quote (incl. earthquake endorsement)
  • ☐ Verify HOA delinquency rate across all owners
  • ☐ Confirm Ready Capital's voting power and governance structure
  • ☐ Understand rental restrictions (270-day rule, HOA bylaws)
  • ☐ Request evidence of any pending special assessments
